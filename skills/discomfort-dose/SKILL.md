# /discomfort-dose

Comfort is a slow leak. The body and mind that do not get stressed deliberately become unable to handle stress when it is not voluntary.

This is not about suffering for its own sake. It is about maintaining the capacity to suffer well, because life will eventually require it.

One voluntary hard thing per week. That is the dose.

## The menu

Pick ONE for this week. Not three. One.

### Physical

- **Cold exposure.** 2-3 minutes in a cold shower or plunge. Start with cold water at the end of a normal shower. Work up.
- **Long fasted training.** A 60+ minute Zone 2 session, fasted. The body learns to burn fat.
- **Ruck.** Put 30-40 lbs in a backpack and walk for an hour. Cheap, effective, outdoor.
- **The one session you dread.** Heavy leg day, hill sprints, whatever you've been skipping.
- **A full day with no caffeine.** If you drink coffee daily, this is surprisingly hard.

### Mental

- **90-minute phone-free deep work block.** No input. Just output. For one unglamorous task.
- **30-minute silence block.** No phone, no music, no input. Sit or walk. Most people cannot.
- **A book chapter a day that you are avoiding.** Dense, old, slow. Aurelius. The Odyssey. A theology book. Something that does not go down easy.
- **A hard topic you have been Googling around.** Sit with it. Write what you think. Share with someone.

### Emotional

- **The postponed hard conversation.** See `/hard-conversation`. Have it this week.
- **The repair you owe.** See `/repair`. Do it today.
- **Reach out to the person you are avoiding.** The old friend you drifted from. The mentor who you stopped returning calls from. The family member who is annoyed with you. Make the call.

### Delay

- **24-hour delay on something you want.** A purchase, a meal, a message. Delay it.
- **No alcohol for the week.** If you drink regularly.
- **No social media for the week.** All apps off the phone.
- **No news for the week.** Full abstention.

## Step 1: Ask

Ask the user: "Which of those sounds hardest for you, this week?"

Whatever they say, that is the answer. The thing you resist is the thing you need.

## Step 2: Define it specifically

Hard things do not happen when they are vague. Get specific:

- "Cold shower" → "The last 2 minutes of my shower, Monday through Friday, at 60°F or colder."
- "Hard conversation" → "The talk with my brother about last Thanksgiving. Sunday afternoon. In person."
- "No phone after 9 p.m." → "Phone in the kitchen drawer by 9 every night this week."

Write it where you will see it. Put it on the calendar.

## Step 3: Commit publicly (optional but powerful)

Tell one person. Text a friend. "This week I'm doing X." External accountability raises follow-through dramatically.

## Step 4: Log after

At the end of the week, one line:

```
~/adulting-data/journal/discomfort-log.md

# <week-of>
Chosen: <...>
Executed: <Y/N>
What it taught me: <one line>
Next week's candidate: <...>
```

## Why this works (the actual case)

Two reasons, one empirical, one philosophical.

Empirical: the evolutionary fitness of the modern adult was built on intermittent stress — hunger, cold, effort, social friction. Absent those stressors, systems (metabolic, vascular, muscular, psychological) degrade. Reintroducing small voluntary stressors keeps the systems tuned.

Philosophical (this is the older frame): if you never practice doing hard things, then when life asks you to do a hard thing (a medical diagnosis, a death, a failure, a crisis) you will be an amateur at suffering. The Stoic idea is that you get better at everything you practice, including discomfort. Practice it small and often. You are buying psychological insurance.

## What this is NOT

- Not Goggins-style bravado. You are not running ultramarathons next month. You are taking a cold shower.
- Not martyrdom. If the hard thing is damaging your health, your family, or your life, stop.
- Not about will. You will fail sometimes. Log it, pick next week's, go.

## The trap

The trap is picking the hard thing that is actually easy for you. A person who trains hard in the gym picking "harder workout" as their hard thing is cheating. The rule: pick what you RESIST. If cold is fine but silence terrifies you, silence is the dose. If physical is fine but vulnerability is hard, vulnerability is the dose.

Ask: "What am I avoiding the most?" That is the answer.
