# /evening-examen

A 5-minute honest review. Seneca said he did this every night; "when the light has been removed and my wife has fallen silent."

## Step 1: Seneca's three questions

Ask the user, one at a time. Capture their answer plainly.

1. **What did I do well today?** Not "what went well" (events). What did *I* do well (action).
2. **What did I do badly?** Be specific. No softening.
3. **What did I leave undone that I should have done?**

## Step 2: The four cardinal virtues

For each, one sentence. Skip any that did not come up today.

- **Courage** — where did I feel fear and move anyway? where did I back down?
- **Discipline** — where did I choose the harder right? where did I cave?
- **Justice** — who did I serve well? who did I fail? was there a small unkindness I did not repair?
- **Wisdom** — what did I see more clearly today? what am I still fooling myself about?

## Step 3: Repair list

> "Is there anything from today that needs a repair tomorrow? A yell at a kid. A snap at a partner. A missed commitment. An ungenerous thought you acted on."

If yes, name it, and commit to the repair by end of day tomorrow. Use `/repair` if helpful.

## Step 4: Gratitude

Three specific, person-linked items. Not "I'm grateful for my family." "I'm grateful that my daughter showed me the Lego car she built today, and I actually looked at it for thirty seconds before answering the text on my phone."

## Step 5: Tomorrow's top 1

One sentence. The most important thing. Do not plan the whole day. One thing.

## Step 6: Output

Append to `~/adulting-data/journal/YYYY-MM-DD-evening.md`:

```
# Evening examen — <date>

## Seneca's three
- Did well: <...>
- Did badly: <...>
- Left undone: <...>

## Virtues
- Courage: <...>
- Discipline: <...>
- Justice: <...>
- Wisdom: <...>

## Repair tomorrow
- <person>: <what>

## Gratitude
1. <specific, person-linked>
2. <specific, person-linked>
3. <specific, person-linked>

## Tomorrow's top 1
<sentence>
```

Print it back. End with one sentence from the user about how the day felt. Then: "Schedule shutdown complete. Phone out of the bedroom."
