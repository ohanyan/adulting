# /partner-review

This is not a review of your partner. It is a review of *you*, as a partner.

Ego will try to make this about them. "They were difficult this week." Irrelevant. You don't get to rate them. You get to rate yourself.

## The five dimensions

Score each 1 (not this week) to 5 (showed up fully). Give a one-sentence reason.

### 1. Presence

- Did I put the phone down when they were talking to me?
- Did I come home from work, or did I bring work home in my body?
- Did I have at least one conversation that was not about logistics?

Score: ___ / 5
Reason: ___

### 2. Invisible labor

- Am I carrying my fair share of the mental load? Kids' schedules, meal planning, house stuff, gifts, social calendar?
- This week, did I initiate any of it, or did I wait to be asked?
- Where do I coast, that they carry?

Score: ___ / 5
Reason: ___

### 3. Initiation

- Did I initiate connection, or only receive it?
- Texts during the day, for no reason. A touch on the way past. Asking how a specific thing went.
- A partner who only receives is not a partner, they are a guest.

Score: ___ / 5
Reason: ___

### 4. Speaking well of them

- How did I speak about them to friends, family, colleagues this week?
- How did I speak about them *to myself*, in my own head?
- Gottman's data: the inner narrative you run about your partner is almost a perfect predictor of where the relationship is headed.

Score: ___ / 5
Reason: ___

### 5. Repair

- Was there a rupture this week, however small? A snap, a dismissal, a phone-scroll while they were trying to connect?
- Did I repair it, or did I hope it would blow over?
- "Repair within 24 hours" is the standard.

Score: ___ / 5
Reason: ___

## The two follow-throughs

1. **One thing to repair this week.** If there is something unrepaired, either do it today with `/repair`, or schedule it by tomorrow end of day.

2. **One gesture for the coming week.** In their love language. On the calendar.

## The honest question

If your partner filled out this rubric about you, would they score you the same way?

If you scored yourself higher than they would, that gap is the real diagnosis. Humility is the only move.

## Output

Append to `~/adulting-data/journal/partner-review.md`:

```

Presence:            <score>/5 — <reason>
Invisible labor:     <score>/5 — <reason>
Initiation:          <score>/5 — <reason>
Speaking well of:    <score>/5 — <reason>

Total: <sum>/25

Repair owed: <what, to do by when>
Gesture this week: <what, when>
```

End with one sentence: "The goal is not a 25. The goal is a trend."
