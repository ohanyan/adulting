# /accountability-mirror

Stand in front of a mirror if you can. Not joking. The practice loses something on a screen.

This is a short, uncomfortable, important ritual. 10 minutes.

## The four questions

Ask the user each of these out loud. They answer, you capture.

1. **What am I avoiding?**
   - Name the one conversation, decision, workout, task, or fact about my life that I am actively not looking at.

2. **Where am I lying to myself?**
   - Where is the story I tell nicer than the data? "I'm getting enough sleep." "I'm present with my kids." "I'm in shape." "I still make time for friends." Where does the story not match the calendar?

3. **What standard am I letting slip?**
   - Something I used to hold myself to that I have quietly renegotiated. Health, work quality, how I speak to my partner, what time I get up. Name the drift.

4. **What is the one corrective action I commit to this week?**
   - One. Not five. Specific. Observable. Scheduled.

## Constraints on the answers

- No "I should." Either "I will, by <date>," or strike it.
- No blame of others. The mirror is for you.
- No wallowing. This is a 10-minute tool, not a therapy session. If something needs more than 10 minutes, note it and consider professional support.

## Output

Append to `~/adulting-data/journal/mirror-log.md`:

```
# <date>

Avoiding: <...>
Lying to self about: <...>
Standard slipping: <...>
Corrective action this week: <...>
Due by: <specific day>

## Last week's commitment
<did I do it? Y/N. if N, why not?>
```

If the user had a commitment from last week that they did not do, do NOT let it slide. Ask:
- Was it the wrong commitment?
- Was it the wrong size?
- Was there a real obstacle, or did you just not do it?

Then they set this week's commitment with eyes open.

End with one line: "Put the action on the calendar before you close this."
