# /love-map-refresh

Gottman's research is blunt: couples who thrive know each other's inner worlds in real time. Not the big things once a year. The small things, this week. The colleague who annoyed them on Tuesday. The podcast they started. The kid they're worried about.

The "Love Map" is the running model of your partner's current inner life. It goes stale fast.

## The self-quiz

Answer each question. If you don't know, don't guess. An honest "I don't know" is the whole point of this exercise.

### Work
1. What project or decision is on their mind this week?
2. Is there a colleague who is either energizing or draining them right now?
3. What are they proud of from the last month?
4. What are they frustrated by?

### Inner life
5. What worry have they mentioned in the last week?
6. What small hope or curiosity?
7. What are they currently reading, watching, or listening to?
8. What topic has come up repeatedly in their conversation lately?

### Relationships
9. Which friend did they last see or hear from?
10. Is there anyone in their family they are actively thinking about or worried about?
11. Which of your shared friends are they closest to right now?

### Body and health
12. Have they been sleeping well?
13. Any physical thing they have mentioned? (Back, knee, stomach, headache.)
14. Are they training, and how do they feel about it?

### The kids (if applicable)
15. For each kid, what is the current "big thing" for that child? School, friend, feeling?
16. Which kid is taking more of your partner's worry this week?

### The two of you
17. When was the last time you laughed together?
18. When was the last time you did something novel together? (Not the usual restaurant.)
19. What is the last thing they asked you for that you haven't acted on?

## Scoring

If you got 15+ you are doing the work. 10-14 means you have drifted. Under 10 means you are roommates.

Either way: no lecture, no shame. This is a diagnostic, not a verdict.

## The refresh

Pick the 3 questions where you were least sure. Over the next week, find an opening to gently, not-as-an-interview, ask about each. The opening might be a drive, a walk, a glass of wine after kids are down.

Do not say "I'm doing a love map refresh." Say: "Hey, I realized I don't know much about how the Jane situation is playing out. How's that going?"

## Love languages (bonus)

Gary Chapman's five love languages:
- Words of affirmation
- Acts of service
- Quality time
- Physical touch
- Gifts

Ask the user: what is your partner's primary? Secondary? Now ask: what is YOURS? Mismatches are where a lot of missed connection happens. You are buying flowers; they want you to put the phone down. You are doing chores; they want to hear the words.

If you don't know their language, the question this week is: "If you had to pick one, what makes you feel most loved?"
