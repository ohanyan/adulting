# /weekly-review

The single highest-leverage hour of the week. Skip it and the system breaks in about three weeks.

## Setup

- Phone in another room.
- 60 to 90 minutes blocked.
- Cup of coffee or tea.
- `~/adulting-data/journal/weekly-review-YYYY-MM-DD.md` open.

If the user says they don't have the time, ask: "What would you be doing instead that is more important than deciding what next week is for?"

## Phase 1: Clear (15 minutes)

Empty every inbox:

1. Email inboxes (all of them).
2. Notes app, Apple Notes, random text files.
3. Voice memos.
4. Physical papers on desk.
5. Browser tabs.
6. The back of your mind. Ask: "What have you been carrying that you have not written down?"

Everything becomes one of:
- **Next action** (a physical thing you can do).
- **Project** (anything that takes more than one action).
- **Waiting-for** (you owe nothing, someone else owes you something; track it).
- **Someday** (not now, not dead).
- **Reference** (file it).
- **Trash**.

If it takes under 2 minutes, do it now.

## Phase 2: Reflect (15 minutes)

Scan last week's calendar. For each day ask silently:
- Did I spend the day on what I said mattered?
- Where did my time actually go?

Then answer, in writing:

1. **Three wins this week.** Be specific.
2. **Three misses this week.** Be honest. No rationalization.
3. **Three lessons.** What did I learn about the work, about people, about myself?
4. **What did I avoid this week?** (The avoided thing is often the most important thing.)

## Phase 3: Roles and goals (Covey, 15 minutes)

List your roles. A typical list:
- Self (health, learning, soul)
- Partner
- Parent (one line per kid)
- Son / daughter (parents)
- Brother / sister / friend
- Professional role 1 (e.g., CEO of X)
- Professional role 2 (if applicable)
- Citizen / community

For each role, ask: "What is the one thing I could do in the next 7 days that would move this forward?"

This is where most people drop the plot. The CEO who has no goal for "partner" or "son" this week is the CEO who will wake up in 10 years shocked.

## Phase 4: Big rocks (10 minutes)

Open next week's calendar. *Before* meetings and work tasks fill it, schedule:

- Training blocks (see `/training-plan`).
- Deep work blocks (one per day, minimum 90 minutes).
- Partner check-in (see `/partner-checkin`).
- 1:1 time per kid (see `/kid-oneonone`).
- Weekly review for next week.
- Any tired-future-self favors (gym bag prepped, meals prepped).

If you do not put these in first, they will not happen. The calendar is the truth, the to-do list is a wish.

## Phase 5: Virtue audit (5 minutes)

From Ryan Holiday's cardinal virtues:

- **Courage:** where did I avoid this week? what hard conversation am I postponing?
- **Discipline:** where did I let a standard slip?
- **Justice:** who did I fail, and what is the repair?
- **Wisdom:** what did I see more clearly this week? what am I still wrong about?

## Phase 6: The four killer questions

End with these. One sentence each:

1. **What am I avoiding?**
2. **What is the one week-making task?**
3. **Who needs a message from me?**
4. **What would make next week a 10 out of 10?**

## Output

Write `~/adulting-data/journal/weekly-review-YYYY-MM-DD.md` with all sections above. Then print back:

- The top 3 outcomes for next week.
- The week-making task.
- Any calendar holds to book now.
- Any repairs owed.

Ask the user: "Any of these you want help drafting or scheduling?" Offer to dispatch `/partner-checkin`, `/hard-conversation`, or `/repair` as follow-ons.
