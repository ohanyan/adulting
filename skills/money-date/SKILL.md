# /money-date

Money stress in relationships is not mostly about income. It is about alignment, transparency, and shared goals. Couples who talk about money quarterly have much lower financial stress than couples who never do.

Run this once per quarter. 60 minutes total. Solo first, then together.

## Part 1: Solo review (30 min)

### Step 1: The numbers

Pull:
- Net worth (assets minus liabilities). A ballpark is fine.
- Last quarter's income (all sources).
- Last quarter's spending, by category.
- Retirement / investment account totals.
- Emergency fund balance.
- Debt balances.

If you do not have a monthly net worth number, take 15 minutes to set up Monarch, YNAB, Copilot, or an old-school spreadsheet. Not optional.

### Step 2: The Conscious Spending Plan (Ramit Sethi)

Divide the most recent quarter's net income into four buckets:

1. **Fixed costs:** rent/mortgage, utilities, insurance, debt minimums, groceries, core transportation. Target: 50-60%.
2. **Investments:** 401k, IRA, brokerage. Target: 10%+.
3. **Savings:** emergency, vacation, specific goals. Target: 5-10%.
4. **Guilt-free spending:** restaurants, travel, hobbies, clothes, everything else. Target: 20-35%.

Compute your actuals. Compare to target.

- If fixed costs are over 60%, you have a structural problem. Housing is usually the lever.
- If investments are under 10%, fix automation today. See Step 4.
- If guilt-free is over 35% AND investments are under 10%, you are underinvesting relative to lifestyle.

### Step 3: The money dials (Ramit)

Which 2-3 spending categories do you love? Travel with family, books, good food, health, a sport, music, something specific.

Turn those dials UP. Spend extravagantly there, without apology.

Which categories do you spend in out of habit or convention, but do not actually love? Cut mercilessly. Ramit's example: some people love fancy cars. Others drive a 2012 Honda and don't care. Do not live in the middle on everything.

Write the 2-3 categories you are consciously dialing UP this quarter.

### Step 4: Automation check

Is your money moving on its own?

- Paycheck → checking account (payday).
- Day 2: auto-transfer to 401k / IRA / brokerage / savings sub-accounts.
- Day 3-5: bills auto-pay from checking.
- What's left in checking = guilt-free money.

If any of those is manual, fix it this weekend. Automation is the difference between people who hit their goals and people who don't.

### Step 5: The "enough" check (Housel)

Write one sentence. Not for right now — for your life. "I have enough when ___."

Examples:
- "I have enough when I can fund my kids' college, own my primary home outright, and have 25x our annual expenses invested."
- "I have enough when I can take a year off without financial stress."
- "I have enough when I don't think about money before making a small decision."

Then look at your life. How close are you? What does "close" even mean for you? If you have enough, why are you still running the treadmill? If you don't yet, what is the path?

### Step 6: Margin of safety (Housel)

- Emergency fund: 6-12 months expenses in liquid cash? If no, this is the top fix.
- Insurance: disability, term life (if dependents), umbrella. Reviewed recently?
- What single event would break you? Do you have a plan for it?

### Step 7: Upcoming

Identify big upcoming expenses:
- Tuition.
- Cars.
- Home repairs.
- Travel.
- Gifts (holidays, weddings).
- Taxes.

Put each in a sinking fund (a savings sub-account) with a monthly transfer so the big expense doesn't surprise you.

## Part 2: Partner money date (30 min)

(If you have a partner. If not, skip.)

### Ground rules

- Same time and place each quarter if possible.
- Phones away.
- Not at the end of a long day. Weekend morning, coffee in hand, is better.
- No "you spent too much on X." This is not the fight. This is the plan.

### The agenda

1. **How do we feel about money right now?** Each of you, honestly. Stressed, fine, uncertain, excited. Name it.
2. **Review the numbers from Part 1.** Net worth, spending by bucket, investments. No judgment, just facts.
3. **Are we aligned on the money dials?** What are we each dialing up this quarter? Do we agree? If not, discuss — not to "win," but to genuinely understand what makes the other person's eyes light up.
4. **The enough statement.** Read each other's "I have enough when ___" sentence. Are they compatible? This question matters enormously and most couples have never asked it.
5. **Big upcoming decisions.** House, car, school, career shift, starting a business, a gift, a trip. What's in the next 6-12 months?
6. **One thing each.** What is one thing you want to change about how you/we handle money this quarter?

### What not to do

- Do not make this a blame session. Money is emotional and shame-loaded. Meet each other with MGI (most generous interpretation).
- Do not hide numbers. The whole point is transparency.
- Do not promise fixes under pressure. If something requires a decision, schedule a dedicated follow-up, not a rushed yes on the spot.

## The philosophical note

Money is a tool. The question is what it is a tool FOR.

If you do not know what you are building toward, more money will not bring more meaning. If you do know, money bought right can give you years of your life back, time with people you love, and the freedom to work on what matters.

The quarterly money date is how a couple finds out, four times a year, whether they are still pointing in the same direction.
