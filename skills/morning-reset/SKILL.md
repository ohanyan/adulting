# /morning-reset

Run this before email, before news, before anything reactive.

## Step 1: Marcus Aurelius reminder (30 seconds)

Print this aloud, or read it slowly:

> "Today I will meet the meddler, the ungrateful, the arrogant, the deceitful, the envious, the unsociable. All of them are these things because they have not learned the difference between good and evil. But I, who have studied the nature of the good and seen it is beautiful, and the nature of the bad and seen it is ugly, cannot be harmed by any of them."
> — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 2.1

## Step 2: Premeditatio malorum (2 minutes)

Ask the user, and capture their one-line answer to each:

1. **What could go wrong today?** Name the most likely three obstacles. Person, event, or internal (fatigue, frustration, a craving).
2. **How would a person of virtue respond?** One sentence per obstacle.
3. **Which cardinal virtue will today demand most?** Courage, discipline, justice, or wisdom? Pick one.

## Step 3: The one thing (1 minute)

> "What is the single outcome that, if accomplished, would make today a win, even if nothing else goes right?"

Write it down. One sentence. Not a todo list. The week-making task from the weekly review may be the answer.

## Step 4: The day's hard thing (McRaven / Goggins)

> "What is the first uncomfortable task on the list, and will you do it before 9am?"

If yes, commit. If no, negotiate a specific earlier-than-usual time.

## Step 5: The people (Love Map + Bids)

> "Who in your house is carrying something today that you should know about?" 
> "What is one bid for connection you will turn toward, not away from, today?"

A bid is any small attempt at attention, affection, or connection. A toddler pointing at a bug. A partner mentioning a colleague. A friend texting a link. Turning toward is the marriage.

## Step 6: Output

Write a short morning page to `~/adulting-data/journal/YYYY-MM-DD-morning.md`:

```
# Morning page — <date>

## Premeditatio
- Obstacle 1: <x> → virtue response: <y>
- Obstacle 2: <x> → virtue response: <y>
- Obstacle 3: <x> → virtue response: <y>

## Virtue to rehearse
<courage | discipline | justice | wisdom> — because <why today>.

## The one thing
<sentence>

## The hard thing, before 9am
<sentence>

## People I will notice
- <name>: <what they are carrying>
- One bid I will turn toward: <what>
```

Print it back to the user. Then stop. Do not give advice or commentary unless asked.
