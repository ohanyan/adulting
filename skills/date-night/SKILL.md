# /date-night

Esther Perel's core insight: domestic love and erotic love pull in opposite directions. Routine is great for raising kids and running a household. It is terrible for desire.

The fix is not grand gestures. It is small deliberate novelty, monthly minimum.

## The rules

1. **Not the usual place.** Break the restaurant rut. New neighborhood, new cuisine, new experience.
2. **Phones away.** Not face-down. Physically away. Backpack, bag, coat pocket zipped.
3. **No logistics talk.** No calendar. No kids' school stuff. No house. No money, unless it's Rich Life dreaming.
4. **No gripe review.** If there are fights to be had, save them for a structured time. This is not it.
5. **Ask real questions.** See below.

## Step 1: Pick the novelty

Ask the user: "What's one thing you've never done together, or haven't done in years, that you could do this month?"

Categories:
- **New experience:** axe throwing, pottery class, cooking class, jazz club, comedy, planetarium, art gallery, botanical garden.
- **New food:** a cuisine neither of you has had. Ethiopian, Georgian, Sichuan, Peruvian.
- **New place:** a neighborhood you never go to. A walkable downtown an hour away.
- **New activity:** hike, bike, roller skate, tennis, kayak, ice skate, pickleball.
- **Nostalgic novelty:** something you used to do when you first met, that you have not done in years.
- **Creation:** do something together that makes an object. Pottery, a cocktail class, plant a garden bed, build a thing.

Pick one. Book it. Put it on the calendar. Make the reservation.

## Step 2: Logistics a human has to handle

- Childcare, if kids. Book it now. Do not leave "figure out the sitter" until the day of.
- Transport. Who drives? Uber? A walk?
- Outfit. If this is a place where effort shows, make the effort. Dressing up is itself novelty.
- Tell the kids you have a date. It models something important for them.

## Step 3: The questions

Print a small handful of real questions. Use any, not all. Pick based on what feels authentic.

Light:
- What is a thing you're into right now that I probably don't know about?
- If you could take a month off from literally everything, what would you do?
- What's a memory from when we first met that I've probably forgotten?

Medium:
- What's been the best month of our marriage / relationship so far, and why?
- What's something you want more of in your life right now?
- What do you wish I asked you about more?

Deeper (use sparingly, only if the vibe is right):
- What are you afraid of these days?
- What do you long for?
- When you imagine us at 70, what does that picture look like?

## Step 4: The forbidden list

Do not, at dinner:
- Work through the kids' school calendar.
- Do the grocery list.
- Call the contractor back.
- Scroll your phone "real quick."
- Reply to the work text "it'll take two seconds."

If something like this tries to happen, say: "We're on our date. It can wait."

## Step 5: The close

End the night with:
- A walk, even 10 minutes, if the weather is decent.
- Genuine eye contact.
- A 20-second hug. Real one.
- One sentence: "Thanks for this. You still surprise me." Or whatever your honest version is.

## Step 6: Book the next one

Before you go to bed, put the next date on the calendar. Even if it's "something, a month from now, we'll pick closer to."

```
Next date: <date, what>
```

## For the nights when the vibe isn't there

Sometimes you go to the trouble and one or both of you is tired, stressed, a little off. That is fine. The magic is not guaranteed. The ritual is.

The ritual tells your relationship: "We still prioritize each other." That is the message. The magic is a bonus.

Do not force it. Be kind to each other when you are both a little flat. Leave early. Get ice cream. Go to bed. Try again in a month.
